About

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Materials Science & Engineering at Dartmouth's Thayer School of Engineering, where I research sustainable solid-state sodium-ion batteries, applying engineering principles to improve battery performance, durability, and safety.

My background spans battery R&D at Storagenergy Technologies, nuclear materials research at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, and ceramics processing at the University of Utah. I'm currently applying machine learning to materials screening and battery diagnostics, and working as a Sourcing Intern at Rhapsody Venture Partners and a Regional NSF I-Corps Fellow with the goal of translating hard-science research into real-world ventures.

Projects

Current Ph.D. Research

Sustainable Solid-State Sodium Batteries

Investigating fluid dynamics and heat transfer principles in solid-state sodium-ion battery design to improve performance, durability, and safety whcich is the core of my Ph.D. research at Dartmouth.

Machine Learning

PFAS Regulatory Risk Classifier

A rules engine plus Random Forest classifier, built in MATLAB, that screens Na-ion battery electrolyte formulations against PFAS, EU Battery Regulation, and critical-materials supply-chain risk. Working on producing a 0–10 risk score with a plain-English breakdown. Building as a customer-discovery demo for NSF I-Corps.

In Progress

Small-Data Materials Discovery Pipeline

A discovery → prediction → performance-linkage → validation pipeline for materials screening, scaled for a single researcher with a modest dataset and synthesis budget rather than an industrial-scale program. The goal is to show this architecture is viable and valuable even at small-data, single-researcher scale.

In Progress

Predicting Battery Lifespan from Early-Cycle EIS

Training a Random Forest / Gaussian Process Regression model on electrochemical impedance spectra from cycles 1–10, bulk resistance, charge-transfer, and diffusion signatures to predict capacity retention at 500 cycles, using characterization data the lab already collects as a byproduct of routine testing.

Earlier Research

Freeze-Cast Ceramic Filters for Radioactive Waste

Researched and fabricated freeze-cast ceramic filters at the University of Utah, using SEM, mechanical testing, and statistical analysis to improve sample quality. Presented at two research conferences..
